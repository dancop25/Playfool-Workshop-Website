About

Cultivate a new creative mindset by renewing your curiosity and observation skills through a series of exercises based on practice and research of designers.

As the world is becoming increasingly complex, companies and organisations continue to seek innovate new solutions. This is why in recent years we’re seeing more and more methodologies to introduce various industries to the design process, such as design thinking. However, even with a thorough framework, good results cannot be guaranteed without also focusing on the creative mindset of the individuals involved.

Dutch historian Johan Huizinga suggested in his book Homo Ludens that the instinct for play is the central element in human culture. In order to adopt an innovative approach, a creative mindset is vital. Like a child’s approach to play, a creative mindset means thinking outside the box, thinking from an unusual perspective and creating unique associations. By embracing play, we can re-awaken the dormant creativity in all of us.

The PLAYFOOL Workshop was developed to inspire new creative ways of looking at things, applying a series of playful exercises and toolkits. The workshop was developed in collaboration between the creative unit Studio PLAYFOOL from the Royal College of Art (RCA) and Yuki Anzai, (Project Assistant Professor, University of Tokyo/CEO, Mimicry Design Inc.) a leading researcher in workshop design.

By taking part in the Facilitator Programme, you will be able to cultivate the creativity of your team and clients, facilitating innovative ideation sessions as a certified PLAYFOOL Workshop Facilitator.

Since its launch, the PLAYFOOL Workshop has been well received in Japan and has been awarded the prestigious Good Design Award in 2018.

Uniqueness

Practice unique exercises based on the latest workshop design theories.

1. Naturally free your mind from everyday thinking through a workshop design based on the latest theory

The exercises of the PLAYFOOL Workshop were developed by applying research from the field of creative cognitive science and innovation management. Each exercise is filled with mechanisms to creatively break out from everyday thinking, such as analogous thinking, conceptually combining contrasting ideas, improvisation and storytelling.

2. Equip a creative mindset through unique exercises

The PLAYFOOL Workshop consists of four original exercises, each with an accompanying tool. Inspire your creativity through each playful and unique exercise, such as creating characters by experimenting with your surroundings, or exploring the environment with special lenses like a detective.

3. Joining the certified PLAYFOOL Workshop Facilitator community

Those who participate in the Facilitator Training Programme will become certified to practice the PLAYFOOL Workshop. A facilitation guidebook as well as additional information and tips on how to effectively hold the the workshop with teams and clients will be provided. Explore and discover your PLAYFOOL Workshop while practicing in your own style.

Programme

Learn through practice and theory to become a certified PLAYFOOL Facilitator in our 2 day programme.

Day 1: PLAYFOOL Workshop

In day 1, experience the four exercises of the PLAYFOOL Workshop

Equip the workshop’s theory and mindset to think of ideas more easily by interacting with the environment in unusual ways. For example, bring your surroundings to life through storytelling or apply analogous thinking to visually combine different objects.

The PLAYFOOL Workshop values the ability to naturally think playfully through the tools developed for each exercise.

At the end of the workshop—when the playfulness is still fresh—there a lecture is held regarding the theoretical side of the workshop. This lecture is aimed to further deepen the understanding of the underlying principles of the PLAYFOOL Workshop, addressing the academic context of design-applied innovation, as well as the correlation between human cognitive ability and creativity.

Day 2: Facilitation Training

In day 2, you will learn how to practice as a PLAYFOOL Workshop Facilitator.

Gain practitioner level skill by experiencing facilitation through role-play using the guidebook, which explains each exercise step-by-step.

In addition to communicating the crucial points of the workshop, we also provide continuous feedback on facilitation to the participants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I participate in the PLAYFOOL Workshop outside of Japan?

Currently the PLAYFOOL Workshop Facilitator Programme is held only in Japan, however there are plans to expand the programme internationally in the near future.

Do I need any previous workshop or facilitation experience?

In this workshop, the aim is to renew everyone’s creative thinking naturally so there is no need for special or prior preparations. For facilitation, an additional facilitation guide will be provided to support you.

Studio PLAYFOOL

Daniel Coppen & Saki Maruyama

Studio PLAYFOOL is a creative unit run by Daniel Coppen and Saki Maruyama, graduates from the Royal College of Art, UK. They create internationally-recognised products and experiences that embrace a core theme of play. Their work includes ‘HALF/FULL’, a set of tableware designed to alter our everyday diets to future proof our appetites, and ‘Lulu, the little lamp that learns’, a lamp which applies machine learning in a new way. Those work have been recognised and awarded internationally.
